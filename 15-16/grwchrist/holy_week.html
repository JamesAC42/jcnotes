Palm Sunday

  • Sunder before Easter
    • Jesus accompanied by his disciples entered Jerusalem in triumph; enthusiastic crowd spread palms on the road and hailed Jesus as the “Son of David”

Tridduum (Three Days)

  • Matthew, Mark, and Luke focus on the institution of the Lord’s Supper; John focuses on the final teachings of Jesus, including the washing of the feet
  • Altar is removed of everything and prepared for Good Friday - symbolic of a tomb slab

Good Friday

  • Marked by silence - no mass is celebrated
  • Meditations centered on Christ’s seven last phrases
  • Peculiar to English language (German word meaning mourning); could be lost in translation from God’s Friday to Good Friday (no tragedy, not even death, can overwhelm God’s providence, love and grace)

7 last phrases

  • Father, forgive them for they know not what they do
  • Today you will be with me in paradise
  • Woman, behold your son
  • My God, My God, why have you forsaken me?
  • I thirst
  • It is finished
  • Father into your hands I commend my spirits

How was Jesus’ death different

  • Crown of thorns
  • Whipping
  • Carried his cross (close to 200 pounds; probably 1 mile up a slight incline)
  • Wood was rough (like a telephone pole)
  • Given hyssop (mulled wine that created dry mouth)
  • Nailed to cross as opposed to being tied
  • Nailed through wrists not palms (palms could not bear the weight)
  • Did not have a foot rest
  • Side was pierced with a lance
  • Death was quick (3 hours as opposed to 3 days)

Holy Saturday

  • Paschal Candle is lit
  • Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults (Baptism for converts) is part of this celebration
  • Alleluia is reinstated

Resurrection

  • Explanation of empty tomb
    • Jesus was unconscious
    • Women went to wrong tomb
    • Thieves stole Jesus’ body
    • Disciples stole Jesus’ body
    • Religious leaders stole Jesus’ body
  • Evidence against
    • Roman soldier told Pilate he was dead; side pierced with a lance; body wrapped and placed in tomb
    • Mary Magdalene and Jesus’ mother saw him placed in tomb; Peter went to same tomb
    • Tomb was sealed and guarded by soldiers
    • Disciples were ready to die for their faith - stealing the body would have been admitting their faith was meaningless Religious leaders would have produced the body to stop rumors of the resurrection
