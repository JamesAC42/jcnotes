Acts of the Apostles

  • Lukes gospel of the Holy Spirit

Background

  • Written by Luke, addressed to Theophilus, written between 80-90 AD
  • Deals with two major apostles - Peter and Paul
  • Recounts two minor apostles - Stephen and Phillip
  • Luke provides a broad survey of church's development and early history (from Jesus to imprisonment of Paul)
  • The town where disciples were first called Christians was Antioch
  • Gentile Christians had to do two things
    • Abstain from meat
    • Avoid relationships that would be considered incestuous

Paul

  • Second missionary journey was to the continent of Europe (accompanied by Luke)
  • Paul worked as a tentmaker to help support himself
  • Luke's plot ends in capital of civilized world - Rome
  • Had to go beyond own culture to spread word

Peter

  • Acts 4:5-12, Peter stands up before the unbelieving rulers and elders and boldly proclaims his faith in Jesus as healer and Messiah
  • The Apostles creed is a shorter version of the Nicene Creed

Holy Spirit

  • You will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes upon you and you will be my witness to the ends of the Earth (explains how Christianity spread under direction of Holy Spirit
  • First verses are a biblical story associated with the sacrament of confirmation
  • Pentecost - 50 days after Easter - celebrates the unity of all Christians

Miscellaneous

  • 3 women were part of the first Christian community - devoted to prayer and charity towards poor
  • Lydia was Paul's first convert in Phillipi; Priscilla accompanied Paul to Antioch- women were active in the early church
  • Matthias replaced Judas
