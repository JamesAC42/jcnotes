The Cell Cycle

Chapter 8


Meiosis

  • Organisms that reproduce sexually are made up of two different types of cells
  • Somatic cells
    • Body cells
    • Contain the normal number of chromosomes
    • Called the diploid number (2n)
    • Skin cells, brain cells, etc.
  • Gametes
    • Sex cells
    • Contain half the normal number of chromosomes
    • Called haploid number (n)
    • Sperm cell and ova
  • N = the number of chromosomes in a set
    • 2n is 2 chromosomes in a set
    • polyploid cells > 2 sets of chromomes (ex. 3n)

Gametes

  • Male gamete
    • Sperm; produced in male gonad testes
  • Female gamete
    • Ovum; produced in female gonad ovaries

Overview

  • Ovulation
    • Ovum released from ovary and moved to area where fertilization can occur
    • Fertilization
      • Joining of Sperm and ovum
    • Fertilization occurs in fallopian tube
      • Results in zygote
      • Reestablishes diploid number

Fertilization

  • Fusion of sperm and egg to form zygote
  • Sperm: n = 23
  • Egg: n = 23

chromosomes

  • Organism with diploid number has 2 matching homologues per set; one from mother and other from father
    • Homologous pair
  • Most organisms are diploid
    • Humans- 23 sets of chromosomes and therefore 46 total chromosomes
    • Diploid number for humans is 46

Homologous chromosomes

  • Pair of chromosomes similar in shape and size
    • From mother and father
  • tetrads
    • Pairs; carry genes controlling the same inherited traits
  • locus
    • Position of a gene; in the same position on homologues
  • Humans have 23 pairs of homologous chromosomes
    • 22 pairs of autosomes
      • Do not determine your gender
    • 1 pair of sex chromosomes
  • Homologous pair is called a tetrad because it has 4 chromatids
    • All 4s telomeres are always being reestablished
  • Karyotype
    • the number and visual appearance of the chromosomes in the cell nuclei of an organism or species.

Sex chromosomes

  • The Sex chromosome's code for the sex of the offspring
    • XX = female
    • XY = male
  • Sex chromosome is the 23rd set

Meiosis

  • Process where gametes are produced
  • Diploid reduced to haploid
    • 2n -> n
  • If meiosis did not occur, chromosome number in each generation would double and offspring would die
  • If duplication of DNA did not occur, genetic variation would be greatly reduced
  • Two cell division
    • Meiosis I and II
    • Only one duplication of chromosomes
  • Spermatogenesis
    • Meiosis in males, produces sperm
  • Oogenesis
    • Meiosis in females, produces eggs

Spermatogenesis

  • Primary Spermatocyte
    • 2n = 46
    • Original cell that divides to make gametes
  • Secondary spermatocyte
    • Primary splits in half
      • Meiosis I
    • N = 23
  • Each secondary splits in to 2 haploid cells
    • N still equals 23
    • Meiosis II
    • In the secondary spermatocyte, the two chromatids were considered 1 chromosome. Now each sperm has 1 chromatid, but it is still referred to as one chromosome

Oogenesis

  • Primary Oocyte
    • Secondary Oocyte
      • Egg
      • Polar bodies
    • Polar body
      • Polar bodies
  • Polar bodies die
  • Only one egg is produced
  • At birth, woman has about 400 primary oocytes

Interphase I

  • Chromosomes duplicate (S phase)
    • Each consists of 2 sister chromatids attached at centromeres
  • Centriole pair replicates
  • Nucleus and nucleolus visible

Meiosis I

  • Division reduces chromosome number by 1/2
  • 4 phases
    • Prophase
    • Metaphase
    • Anaphase
    • Telophase

Prophase

  • Longest and most complex
    • 90% of the process
  • Chromosomes condense
  • Synapsis
    • Homologous chromosomes form a tetrad
    • Tetrad is 2 chromosomes or 4 chromatids

Two important events

  • Crossing over
    • Segments of non-sister chromatids break and reattach to the other chromatid
    • Chiasmata - sites of crossing over
    • First and greatest level of genetic diversity
    • |.| * |.| -> X.X
  • Disjunction

Problems such as Downs Syndrome are a result of Non-disjunction

Metaphase

  • Shortest phase
  • Tetrads align on the metaphase plate
  • Independent assortment:
    • Resulting sperm cells' chromosomes that they receive is completely random
    • Orientation of homologous pair to poles is random
    • Variation
    • Formula: 2n
    • Example:
      • 2n = 4
      • N = 2
      • 22 = 4

Anaphase I

  • Homologous chromosomes separate and move towards the poles
  • Sister chromatids remain attached at their centromere

Telophase

  • Each pole has 2 chromosomes
  • Split into 2 sperm

Meiosis II

Prophase II

  • Same as prophase in mitosis

Metaphase II

  • Same as metaphase in mitosis

Anaphase II

  • Same as in mitosis
  • Sister chromatids separate

Telophase II

  • Same in mitosis
  • Nuclei form
  • Cytokinesis occurs
  • Four haploid daughter cells produced
    • In the egg, only one is viable
    • Polar bodies are reabsorbed

Non-disjunction

  • One of the two major occurrences of Meiosis
    • Other is crossing over
  • Failure of homologous pairs, or sister chromatids, to separate during meiosis
  • Results with the production of zygotes with abnormal chromosome numbers
    • An abnormal chromosome number is damaging to the offspring
  • Usually occurs in one of two ways
    • Monosomy - only one chromosome; missing one
    • Trisomy - 3 chromosomes; extra
    • Trisomy 18 : 3 chromosomes in the 18th set
    • Monosomy 23: only one chromosome in 23rd set

Common non-disjunction disorders

  • Downs syndrome - Trisomy 21
  • Turners syndrome - Monosomy 23 (X)
  • Kleinfelters syndrome - Trisomy 23 (XXY)
  • Edwards syndrome - Trisomy 18

Amniocentesis

  • Procedure a pregnant woman can have in order to detect some genetic disorders such as non-disjunction
  • Needle put through stomach into amniotic fluid and fluid is drawn
    • Fluid contains cells of the baby
  • DNA is tested
  • CVS method takes a piece of placenta to test
    • More dangerous but can be performed earlier

Karyotype

  • Picture of individual chromosomes
  • One of the ways to analyze the amniocentesis is to make a karyotype

Mechanisms of sexual reproduction

  • Fertilization
    • Union of sperm and egg
    • Humans have internal fertilization
      • Sperm are deposited in/ near the female reproductive tract, fertilization occurs within the tract

Mechanisms ensure that mature sperm encounter fertile eggs

  • Internal fertilization requires cooperative behavior, leading to copulation
  • Internal fertilization requires sophisticated reproductive systems, including copulatory organs that deliver the sperm and receptacles for its storage and transport to ripe eggs

Species that utilize internal fertilization

  • Internal fertilizers produce fewer zygotes, but provide more parental protection than external fertilizers
    • Protection in humans include development of the embryo within the reproductive tract of the female parent and parental care of the offspring
      • At birth the human is not capable of independent existence

complex reproductive systems

  • Humans have systems that produce gametes and deliver them to the gametes of the opposite sex
    • Gonads - organs that produce gametes
    • Ovaries - eggs
    • Testes - sperm

reproductive anatomy of the Male

  • Scrotum and penis
    • Gonads, hormones, and accessory glands that secrete products essential to sperm movement, and ducts that carry the sperm and glandular secretions
    • Gonad
      • Many highly coiled tubes called seminiferous tubes where sperm forms
    • Scrotum is 2 degrees Celsius cooler than the body temperature
    • From seminiferous tubes the sperm pass into the coiled tubes of the epididymis
    • Epididymis
      • Takes 20 days for sperm to travel 6 meters
      • The sperm becomes motile and gains the ability to fertilize an egg
    • Ejaculation
      • Sperm propelled from epididymis through he vas deferens
      • 2 ducts run from the scrotum and joins forming the ejaculatory duct
      • Duct opens into the urethra which opens to the outside at the tip of the penis

3 sets of accessory glands

  • Seminal vesicle
    • 60% of total volume of semen
      • Mucus, sugar for sperm energy, Vitamin C and prostaglandins
  • Prostate gland
    • Anticoagulant enzymes and citrate (sperm nutrients)
  • Bulbourethral glands
    • Mucus that neutralizes acid urine remaining in the urethra
    • Carries some sperm released before ejaculation, one reason for the high failure of withdrawal method of birth control
