The Cell Cycle

Chapter 8


Meiosis

  • Organisms that reproduce sexually are made up of two different types of cells
  • Somatic cells
    • Body cells
    • Contain the normal number of chromosomes
    • Called the diploid number (2n)
    • Skin cells, brain cells, etc.
  • Gametes
    • Sex cells
    • Contain half the normal number of chromosomes
    • Called haploid number (n)
    • Sperm cell and ova
  • N = the number of chromosomes in a set
    • 2n is 2 chromosomes in a set
    • polyploid cells > 2 sets of chromomes (ex. 3n)

Gametes

  • Male gamete
    • Sperm; produced in male gonad testes
  • Female gamete
    • Ovum; produced in female gonad ovaries

Overview

  • Ovulation
    • Ovum released from ovary and moved to area where fertilization can occur
    • Fertilization
      • Joining of Sperm and ovum
    • Fertilization occurs in fallopian tube
      • Results in zygote
      • Reestablishes diploid number

Fertilization

  • Fusion of sperm and egg to form zygote
  • Sperm: n = 23
  • Egg: n = 23

chromosomes

  • Organism with diploid number has 2 matching homologues per set; one from mother and other from father
    • Homologous pair
  • Most organisms are diploid
    • Humans- 23 sets of chromosomes and therefore 46 total chromosomes
    • Diploid number for humans is 46

Homologous chromosomes

  • Pair of chromosomes similar in shape and size
    • From mother and father
  • tetrads
    • Pairs; carry genes controlling the same inherited traits
  • locus
    • Position of a gene; in the same position on homologues
  • Humans have 23 pairs of homologous chromosomes
    • 22 pairs of autosomes
      • Do not determine your gender
    • 1 pair of sex chromosomes
  • Homologous pair is called a tetrad because it has 4 chromatids
    • All 4s telomeres are always being reestablished
  • Karyotype
    • the number and visual appearance of the chromosomes in the cell nuclei of an organism or species.

Sex chromosomes

  • The Sex chromosome's code for the sex of the offspring
    • XX = female
    • XY = male
  • Sex chromosome is the 23rd set

Meiosis

  • Process where gametes are produced
  • Diploid reduced to haploid
    • 2n -> n
  • If meiosis did not occur, chromosome number in each generation would double and offspring would die
  • If duplication of DNA did not occur, genetic variation would be greatly reduced
  • Two cell division
    • Meiosis I and II
    • Only one duplication of chromosomes
  • Spermatogenesis
    • Meiosis in males, produces sperm
  • Oogenesis
    • Meiosis in females, produces eggs

Spermatogenesis

  • Primary Spermatocyte
    • 2n = 46
    • Original cell that divides to make gametes
  • Secondary spermatocyte
    • Primary splits in half
      • Meiosis I
    • N = 23
  • Each secondary splits in to 2 haploid cells
    • N still equals 23
    • Meiosis II
    • In the secondary spermatocyte, the two chromatids were considered 1 chromosome. Now each sperm has 1 chromatid, but it is still referred to as one chromosome

Oogenesis

  • Primary Oocyte
    • Secondary Oocyte
      • Egg
      • Polar bodies
    • Polar body
      • Polar bodies
  • Polar bodies die
  • Only one egg is produced
  • At birth, woman has about 400 primary oocytes

Interphase I

  • Chromosomes duplicate (S phase)
    • Each consists of 2 sister chromatids attached at centromeres
  • Centriole pair replicates
  • Nucleus and nucleolus visible

Meiosis I

  • Division reduces chromosome number by 1/2
  • 4 phases
    • Prophase
    • Metaphase
    • Anaphase
    • Telophase

Prophase

  • Longest and most complex
    • 90% of the process
  • Chromosomes condense
  • Synapsis
    • Homologous chromosomes form a tetrad
    • Tetrad is 2 chromosomes or 4 chromatids

Two important events

  • Crossing over
    • Segments of non-sister chromatids break and reattach to the other chromatid
    • Chiasmata - sites of crossing over
    • First and greatest level of genetic diversity
    • |.| * |.| -> X.X
  • Disjunction

Problems such as Downs Syndrome are a result of Non-disjunction
