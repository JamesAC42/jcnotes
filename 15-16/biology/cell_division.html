The Cell Cycle

Chapter 8


Multicellular Eukaryotic Cell cycle

  • Cell division - reproduction, growth and repairs
    • Genetics distributed to two daughter cells
  • Genes - total hereditary endowment
  • DNA packaged into chromosomes
    • Human somatic cells - 46 chromosomes - Diploid 2n
    • Reproductive cells (gametes) have 23 chromosomes - Haploid n
  • Chromosomes is 1 very long strand of DNA with thousands of genes
  • Gene - unit that specifies an inherited trait
  • Some voluntary skeletal muscle can have more than one nuclei in a natural state
    • Does not apply to other cells
  • Red blood cells have no nuclei

DNA

  • DNA is associated with various proteins
    • Maintain structure and help control activity of genes
  • DNA-protein complex is called chromatin
  • After a cell duplicates genome in preparation for division, chromatic condenses. Becomes densely coiled and folded making chromosomes so thick that we can see them with a light microscope
  • Each duplicated chromosomes consists of 2 sister chromatids, which contain identical copies of the DNA molecule

Mitosis vs. Meiosis

  • Mitosis
    • Division of nucleus followed by cytokinesis.
    • Cytokinesis is division of the cytoplasm
  • Meiosis
    • Daughter cells only receive 1/2 of the chromosome number
    • Haploid = n
    • Occurs in ovaries and testes

2 sister chromatids

  • Once the S phase has happened and DNA has been replicated
  • One chromosome made of 2 sister chromatids

Karyotype

  • matching the chromosomes visually in the cell
  • Can reveal what kind of organism it belongs

Cell Cycle

  • Mitotic Phase
    • Actual mitosis
    • M phase
    • Broken down into 4 subphases
      • Prophase
      • Metaphase
      • Anaphase
      • Telophase
    • Some biologists break down mitosis into 5 subphases
      • Prophase ,prometaphase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase
      • Essentially break Prophase into 2 subphases
    • Does not matter how we break down each step, as the whole process is continuous
  • Interphase
    • Between cell division (M phase) the cell is in interphase
      • 3 subphases
        • G1 phase - GROWTH
        • S Phase - DNA REPLICATION - copies chromosomes
        • G2 phase - GROWTH AND PREPARATION FOR MITOSIS
    • In all 3 subphases cells grow by producing proteins. Chromosomes are duplicated during the S phase only

Prophase - the mitotic spindle

  • Mitotic spindle forms in cytoplasm during prophase
  • During interphase, the centrosome replicates to form 2, located just outside nucleus.
    • The 2 move farther apart during prophase (prometaphase).
    • A spindle of microtubules grows out of them,
    • By the end of prophase (or prometaphase) the 2 centrosomes are at opposite sides of the cell
  • Each of the 2 chromatids of a chromosome has a kinetichore at the centromere

Metaphase - lined up chromosomes

  • At metaphase the centromeres of all the duplicated chromosomes are on a plane midway between the 2 poles

Anaphase - separation

  • Anaphase commences with the separation of the sister chromatids
  • Cytokinesis
    • Division of the cytoplasm
    • Process of cleavage
    • Cleavage furrow - shallow groove - first sign

DNA Replication

  • During DNA, base pairing enables existing DNA strands to serve as templates for new complementary strands
    • This is called the semi-conservative model
    • Parent model not intact
  • Origin of DNA replication
    • 1000s in eukaryotic DNA strand
    • Multiple bubbles form and eventually fuse
    • Replication proceeds in both direction from origin
    • At each end is a replication fork

DNA Replication

  • DNA polymerase - enzyme catalyst for elongation by aligning nucleotides at t arate of about 50/second
  • Antiparallel strands
    • 5' phosphate -> 3' hydroxyl
    • Leading strand vs lagging strand
  • DNA ligase - joins Okazaki fragments of the lagging strand.
    • Joins the beginning and end of leading strands
  • Primer is not DNA, but rather an RNA primer
    • Enzyme primase joins RNA nucleotide to make primer (10 nucleotides long)
    • Late replaced with DNA
  • Helicase
    • Untwists the double helix
  • Topoisomerase
    • Alter the supercoiling of DNA by relaxing the coil so helicase can work

Proof Reading and Repair

  • Enzymes proof read DNA and repair damage
    • Error rate is 1/10,000 base pairs
  • Repair mechanisms
    • Mismatch repair - fix mistakes during replication. DNA polymerase carries this out
    • Excision Repair - repair to existing DNA - nucleus cuts out damaged strand and fills with properly paired bases
      • DNA polymerase and ligase help with this repair

Telomeres

  • The ends of DNA cause specific problems
    • 5' end is impossible to complete
    • DNA has a special telomere at its end. It does not contain genes. Instead a short multiple repeating sequence of "TTAGGG" Repeated
  • Telomerase - catalyzes the lengthening of telomeres
